## Naming & writing rules
- Product name in prose: **Affluents** (capital A). Wordmark: lowercase `affluents`.
- Buckets are always **Spend / Reserve / Earn** — never rename them poetically.
  River vocabulary (streams, flow, routing, confluence) is allowed in marketing
  copy and section headers, never in transactional UI where clarity rules
  (buttons say "Create payment link", "Withdraw", not "Open a tributary").
- Voice: calm, precise, zero hype. Plain verbs, sentence case, active voice.
  Buttons say exactly what happens; an action keeps its name through the flow
  ("Pay 100 USDC" → "Paid ✓"). Errors state what happened and what to do next;
  they never apologize vaguely. One French wink allowed in the pitch, once.
- Never overclaim: it is automation, not AI; the Demo Vault is labeled
  "Demo Vault — on-chain position", no invented APY.

## Signature element (the one memorable thing)
**The confluence glyph:** three thin streams converge into one channel, then
split into three branches — income → Affluents → Spend/Reserve/Earn. Drawn as a
single-weight line figure; works as favicon, dashboard empty-state, and the
skeleton of the ONE signature animation: when a payment verifies, a line flows
from the payment amount and branches into the three buckets as they fill
(≈600ms, ease-out, respects prefers-reduced-motion). Bonus typographic hook:
the wordmark contains a true **ffl ligature** — a-ffl-uents — three letters
fusing into one form, echoing the confluence.
**Hierarchy (validated):** the confluence glyph is the PRIMARY durable brand
symbol (favicon, app icon, avatar, loading/transaction animation, motif); the
ffl ligature is a SECONDARY typographic signature inside the wordmark. The
identity must never depend on ligature rendering (fonts, weights, and browsers
vary); the wordmark also works in a face without the ligature.

## Three visual directions

### A — "Confluence charts" (RECOMMENDED)
Inspired by bathymetric river charts and French cartographic engraving —
premium, calm, editorial; a private bank that owns an atlas.
- **Palette (cool, deliberately NOT warm-cream fintech):**
  `--mist #F2F4F3` (background) · `--ink #14232A` (text, deep teal-black) ·
  `--river #23617A` (primary accent, links, Spend/EURC) ·
  `--reserve #B8893D` (muted ochre, Reserve) · `--earn #35684F` (deep green, Earn) ·
  `--contour #D9DFDD` (hairlines, chart gridlines)
- **Type:** display **EB Garamond** (French typographic heritage; true ffl
  ligature for the wordmark; used with restraint — wordmark, page titles, the
  big amount on the payment page) · body/UI **Inter** with `tnum` tabular
  figures for every amount. Amounts are typographic heroes: large, tabular,
  6-decimal-truncated to 2 in UI with full precision on hover/detail.
- **Texture:** faint contour-line pattern (SVG, 2–3% opacity) on landing and
  empty states only — never behind data.
- **Light-first.** Light UIs read better in compressed demo video and
  screenshots, and stand apart in a sea of dark crypto dashboards.
- Payment page: mist background, the amount in Garamond at ~72px, one river-blue
  button "Pay 100 USDC", the confluence glyph as a quiet footer mark.

### B — "Ledger" (Swiss financial modernism)
Near-monochrome paper/ink grid, grotesque type only (Inter or IBM Plex Sans
everywhere), zero decoration; the single accent is `--earn` green used ONLY on
money-positive moments. Distinctiveness comes from typographic discipline and
beautiful tabular data. Safest, most "institutional", least memorable.

### C — "Nightflow" (dark, luminous restraint)
Deep slate `#0E1B21` UI, thin luminous flowlines animating along routes.
Demo-wow potential, but this is the default genre look at crypto hackathons
(dark + one glowing accent) and drifts toward the exact clichés we're told to
avoid. Only viable with extreme restraint. Not recommended as primary.

## Recommendation
**Direction A**, borrowing B's numeric/tabular discipline for all data
surfaces. Rationale: it is subject-grounded (rivers, cartography, French
heritage via Garamond), premium-calm per the brief, distinctive against both
neon-crypto and the warm-cream/serif template look, and it films well — the
judges meet this brand in a compressed 3-minute video and static screenshots,
where light editorial design carries.

## Do not
Generic crypto neon; heavy gradients; literal cartoon rivers; playful fintech
blobs; warm-cream + terracotta template look; more than one signature
animation; river metaphors inside transactional UI copy.
